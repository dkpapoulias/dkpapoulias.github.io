2023

  1. Physics implications of a combined analysis of COHERENT CsI and LAr data
    V. De Romeri, O. G. Miranda, D. K. Papoulias, and 3 more authors
    JHEP, 2023 04 , 035
    Abstract DOI arXiv

    The observation of coherent elastic neutrino nucleus scattering has opened the window to many physics opportunities. We perform a detailed statistical analysis of the full COHERENT CsI data and combine it with the previous argon result, discussing implications from tests of the Standard Model to new physics probes. In particular, we update previous measurements of the weak mixing angle and the neutron root mean square charge radius for CsI and argon, taking into account experimental uncertainties associated to efficiency and timing distribution of neutrino fluxes.

2021

  1. Axionlike particles searches in reactor experiments
    D. Aristizabal Sierra, V. De Romeri, L. J. Flores, and 1 more author
    JHEP, 2021 03 , 294
    Abstract DOI arXiv

    Reactor neutrino experiments provide a rich environment for the study of axionlike particles (ALPs). Using the intense photon flux produced in the nuclear reactor core, these experiments have the potential to probe ALPs with masses below 10 MeV. The feasibility of searches via ALPs produced through Primakoff and Compton-like processes as well as nuclear transitions is explored, demonstrating that reactor-based experiments have high potential to test ALP-photon couplings and masses currently probed only by cosmological and astrophysical observations.

2020

  1. Implications of the first detection of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) with Liquid Argon
    O. G. Miranda, D. K. Papoulias, G. Sanchez Garcia, and 3 more authors
    JHEP, 2020 05 , 130
    Abstract DOI arXiv

    The CENNS-10 experiment of the COHERENT collaboration has reported the first detection of coherent-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering in liquid Argon with more than 3σsignificance. Exploiting the new data to probe parameters of key importance to CEvNS within and beyond the Standard Model, we derive a first measurement of the neutron rms charge radius of Argon and an improved determination of the weak mixing angle in the low energy regime. Constraints on neutrino non-standard interactions, electromagnetic properties, and light mediators are also updated.

  2. COHERENT constraints after the COHERENT-2020 quenching factor measurement
    Dimitrios K. Papoulias
    Phys. Rev. D, 2020 102 (11) , 113004
    Abstract DOI arXiv

    An improved quenching factor (QF) measurement for low-energy nuclear recoils in CsI[Na] has been reported by the COHERENT Collaboration. The new energy-dependent QF is characterized by reduced systematic uncertainty and leads to better agreement between the experimental COHERENT data and the Standard Model expectation. Updated constraints on parameters describing coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering within and beyond the SM are reported, along with a discussion of how the new QF affects their interpretation.

2018

  1. COHERENT constraints to conventional and exotic neutrino physics
    D. K. Papoulias and T. S. Kosmas
    Phys. Rev. D, 2018 97 (3) , 033003
    Abstract DOI arXiv

    The process of neutral-current coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering has been recently measured by the COHERENT experiment. On the basis of the observed signal and nuclear calculations for the relevant Cs and I isotopes, constraints on both conventional and exotic neutrino physics are updated. The study concentrates on SM extensions involving vector and tensor nonstandard interactions, neutrino electromagnetic properties, light sterile neutrino states, and scenarios with new propagator fields such as vector Z’ and scalar bosons.
